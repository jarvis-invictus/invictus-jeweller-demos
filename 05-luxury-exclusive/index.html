BIS Hallmarked

Every piece carries the Bureau of Indian Standards hallmark. Our 22K temple jewellery is certified at 916 purity, and our 24K deity ornaments at 999 purity. No exceptions — because what adorns the divine must be pure.

Traditional Techniques

We use Nakashi (repoussé), hand-engraving, and temple wax-casting — the same techniques that created the ornaments in Meenakshi Temple and Tirupati. No machine stamping. No electroforming. Just gold, fire, and the karigar's hands.

Our Artisan Heritage

◆
Since 1989

From Nagercoil & Thrissur to Tulsi Baug

In 1989, Shri Venkatesh Iyer opened a modest workshop near Tulsi Baug with a single vision: to bring the sacred gold traditions of South India to Maharashtra's devotees. His karigars — master goldsmiths trained in the temple workshops of Nagercoil, Tamil Nadu and Thrissur, Kerala — carried with them techniques passed down through five generations.

What started as a small counter serving the Tulsi Baug mandirs has grown into Pune's most trusted name for deity ornaments and bridal temple sets. Today, temples across Maharashtra — from Siddhivinayak trusts to family mandirs — trust Sri Durgamba for their deity shringar.

Our workshop still operates the way it did in 1989: no assembly lines, no mass production. Each piece begins with a prayer and ends with a hallmark — because we believe the goldsmith's devotion lives in the gold.
